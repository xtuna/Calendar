MONTHS OF THE YEAR

HOLIDAYS

    New Year's Day

    The Philippines welcomes the New Year with festive
    celebrations, fireworks, and gatherings. Families and friends
    come together to bid farewell to the old year and welcome the
    new one with hope and optimism.

    Chinese New Year

    Celebrated by the Filipino-Chinese community,
    Chinese New Year marks the beginning of the lunar new year.
    Festivities include dragon and lion dances, traditional rituals,
    and the sharing of meals to usher in good luck and prosperity.

    EDSA People Power Anniversary

    The EDSA People Power Anniversary commemorates the
    peaceful People Power Revolution in 1986, which led to the
    ousting of the Marcos dictatorship. Filipinos celebrate this
    event with ceremonies, exhibits, and reflections on the
    importance of democracy.

    Maunday Thursday

    Maundy Thursday is part of the Holy Week observance.
    Filipinos often participate in religious activities, including
    attending church services and processions. It is a time for
    reflection on the sacrifices of Jesus Christ.

    Good Friday

    Good Friday is a solemn day commemorating the
    crucifixion of Jesus Christ. Many Filipinos engage in acts of
    penance, such as the "Senakulo" (Passion Play) and joining
    processions depicting the Stations of the Cross.

    Black Saturday

    Following Good Friday, Black Saturday is a day of quiet
    reflection and mourning. It precedes the joyous celebration of
    Easter Sunday. Some communities hold solemn vigils and
    religious services.

    Labor Day

    Labor Day recognizes the contributions of workers in the
    Philippines. It is a national holiday marked by workers' rallies,
    parades, and various events advocating for labor rights and
    social justice.

    Independence Day

    Independence Day commemorates the Philippine
    Declaration of Independence from Spanish colonial rule in 1898.
    Festivities include flag-raising ceremonies, parades, and cultural
    programs to honor the country's freedom.

    National Heroes Day

    National Heroes Day pays tribute to the nation's heroes, both
    known and unknown, who sacrificed for Philippine freedom.
    Commemorative events, wreath-laying ceremonies, and
    historical discussions are held nationwide.

    Ninoy Aquino Day

    Ninoy Aquino Day honors the life and legacy of
    Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., a key figure in the fight
    against the Marcos dictatorship. It is a day of remembrance,
    with ceremonies highlighting his contributions to democracy.

    All Saints' Day

    All Saints' Day is a time for Filipinos to remember and honor
    their deceased loved ones. Families visit cemeteries, offer
    prayers, and decorate graves with flowers and candles.

    All Souls' Day

    Following All Saints' Day, All Souls' Day is a continuation of
    remembering and praying for the souls of the departed. It is a
    day of reflection on the concept of life after death.

    Bonifacio Day

    Bonifacio Day commemorates the birth of
    Andres Bonifacio, a key leader in the Philippine Revolution
    against Spanish colonization. Events include wreath-laying
    ceremonies, parades, and discussions on his role in history.

    Feast of the Immaculate Conception of Mary

    The Feast of the Immaculate Conception of Mary,
    observed on December 8th in the Catholic Church, celebrates
    the belief that Mary was conceived without original sin,
    highlighting her purity and holiness. It is a day of special
    devotion honoring the mother of Jesus..

    Christmas Day

    Christmas in the Philippines is marked by extended
    celebrations, colorful decorations, and festive gatherings.
    Filipinos celebrate with family reunions, traditional meals,
    and the well-known "Simbang Gabi" (Night Mass).

    Rizal Day

    Rizal Day honors the life and works of Dr. Jose Rizal,
    a national hero and key figure in the Philippine Revolution
    against Spanish rule. Commemorative activities include
    ceremonies, educational events, and reflections on his writings.
